Critical Temperature - When Does Bonding Fail?

Estimates the temperature at which thermal stress exceeds the bonding/fracture limit during cooldown from 300 K.
Formula: T_critical = T_room − sigma_limit / (α_eff · E_eff)
Note: This assumes linear α(T). Real α(T) decreases at low T → T_critical is a conservative upper bound.

E_eff: effective in-plane modulus of LiNbO₃ at current orientation (from rotated stiffness tensor C'₁₁₁₁)
α_eff: effective mismatch expansion coefficient = |α_Sap − α_LN_inplane|
sigma_limit options: 23 MPa (bond), sigma_fracture_LN (bulk), sigma_yield_In (In layer)

About This Tool

LiNbO₃ / Sapphire Cryogenic Stress Calculator
ANISOTROPIC THERMOELASTIC ANALYSIS · V2.0 · RESEARCH TOOL
A specialized open-source calculator for designing cryogenic bonded structures based on LiNbO₃, LiTaO₃, and ScAlN piezoelectric materials. Computes full anisotropic thermoelastic stress tensors, identifies design limits, and provides In-layer relaxation and ScAlN optimization guidance for quantum device applications operating at 4 K.

Research Background & Key Findings
Finding 1
Magic-cut does not exist.
Minimum stress is 545.7 MPa (X-cut),
24x over the bond limit of 23 MPa.
Finding 2
ScAlN (Sc 36%) on Sapphire:
sigma = 19.4 MPa — below bond limit.
K² = 193.6% at room temperature.
Finding 3
Optimal Sc% diverges:
RT optimum = 36% (K² max)
4K optimum = 10–20% (K²×Q max)
