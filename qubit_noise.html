Physical Model & References

Plasmon (DIET) model:

A_eff/A_0 = exp(−k_des0 × |E_gap/E_0|² × t)
|E_gap/E_0|² = |1/(ε(ω) + ε_m(1−Lz)/Lz)|² × (L/gap)^2.5 × 0.3
ε_m = 2.25 (SiO₂), Lz = 0.039 (cylinder AR=4), L = 80 nm
k_des0 calibrated: gap = 1.5 nm, t = 1 h → 10× reduction (de Graaf 2018)

SAW phonon model:

A_ratio = 1 / (1 + coupling_eff × Q × M × t)
M = exp(−(f_SAW − f_TLS)² / 2)
Q = 65,000, f_TLS = 8 GHz, coupling_eff = user-defined (default 1.54×10⁻⁵)

Au Drude model:

ε(ω) = ε_∞ − ω_p² / (ω(ω + iγ))
ε_∞ = 9.0, ω_p = 1.3697×10¹⁶ rad/s, γ(300K) = 1.2288×10¹⁴ rad/s

Physical model and references:
DIET calibration anchor: de Graaf, S.E. et al. Nature Communications 9, 1143 (2018).
Field enhancement model: Gans, R. Ann. Phys. 352, 270 (1915) — NOTE: Gans theory applies to isolated spheroids, NOT dimer nanogaps. The approximation (λ/gap)1.5 is empirical. The depolarization factor LZ=0.039 is hardcoded (documented as cylinder AR=4, but derivation not independently verified). Accurate field enhancement requires FDTD calculation. Note: EPS_M=2.25 (SiO₂) is hardcoded without documented justification; de Graaf 2018 used Al₂O₃ substrate (ε≈ 3.1). L=80 nm reference length is hardcoded without documented justification.
TLS dipole moment range: Sarabi, B. et al. Physical Review Letters 116, 167002 (2016).
Au dielectric constants: Johnson, P.B. & Christy, R.W. Physical Review B 6, 4370 (1972).
Open problem (quantified in this work): de Graaf et al. (2018) removed TLF (slow fluctuators) with switching-rate bandwidth W ~ 100 K (8,600 μeV), where W is limited by the desorption energy (de Graaf 2018, Discussion), while qubits at 15 mK are affected by TLS with E < 1.3 ueV (= kT at 15 mK). This ~6,600x energy scale discrepancy between DIET-removed TLF and qubit-relevant TLS is an unresolved theoretical question identified in this work.
Disclaimer: The SAW phonon coupling_eff is a theoretical estimate, not yet experimentally verified. Users should verify results with their own experimental data before making engineering decisions.
